Wednesday May 2 2001

Hindustan Times New Dehli

 

Was it Ghadar or Chaddar Movement

 

        Call it a typographical error or an ostensible mistake the fact remains that for the past 20 years the Union government still continues to recognize the historical movement as, "Chaddar movement". The brochure published by the Ministry of Home Affairs that was printed in 1980 after the introduction of the Swatantra Sainik Samman pension scheme, 1980, still continues to carry the name of an unheard movement "the Chaddar movement" enlisted at serial number 12 while the name of Ghadar movement gets no mention in the entire list mentioning as many as 30 minor and major mutinies and movements.

 

HTC, Ludhiana

 

HC issues notice to Centre, 2 states

 

Amit Sharma

Ludhiana, May 1

 

        Entertaining a public interest litigation files by a Chandigarh resident to "redeem Ghadarites pride", the Punjab and Haryana High Court has issued notice of motion to the Government of India through the Ministry of Home Affairs and Punjab and Haryana governments through their

respective chief secretaries for September 17, 2001.

        Hearing the petition filed by Mr. Malwinder Jit Singh Waraich, a Chandigarh based researched on the Ghadar movement who appeared in person, the notice of motion was issued by a divisional branch of justice Mr. R S Mongia and justice Mr. K C Gupta on its hearing held on April 24, last.

        On the basis of his research and quoting certain historical facts Mr. Waraich in his petition has sought directions from the court to the Ministry of Home Affairs (GoI) to recognize the Ghadar Movement (1914-1915) which the petitioner alleged was not included in the list of

movements and mutinies recognised by central government besides seeking the orders from the court to the Punjab government to extend the benefits to all the collateral heirs of the Ghadarites as it had already done to the cousin sister of martyr Kartar Singh Sarabha, Mata Jagdish Kaur to

whom it recently accorded the status of "mother of Punjab".

        It had been pointed out that the state government, in a reply to a question on the same issue by comrade Hardev Harshi, a CPI MLA had categorically replied in the recent Assembly session (March 27, 2001) that "only unmarried daughters of Ghadarites were eligible for the

benefits under the pension scheme."

Challenging the same reply of the state government through its minister for the welfare of freedom fighters and jails the petitioner has categorically accused the Punjab government of "misleading" the house and observed that "it ill behoves the Punjab government to practice

arbitrariness in the matters of noble souls of the motherland in recognition of their supreme sacrifices."

        Stating that the "ill conceived" and "unjust" response of the

state government towards the martyrs is "incompatible" with any standards

of governance, Mr. Waraich pleaded before the court that in the interests

of justice the government should grant pension benefits to all collateral

heirs of Ghadarites.

        Maintaining that the "omission" of the Ghadar movement from the

list, which entitles the dependents and the family members of Ghadarites

to the freedom fighter's pension benefits, must be "rectified" the

petitioner alleged that to exclude a movement in which 46 Ghadarites were

hanged, 69 imprisoned for life and transported to Andaman and Nicobar

Islands, 145 awarded lesser sentences and properties of most of these

revolutionaries confiscated after the movement, is a gross violation of

Article 14 of the Constitution of India, besides being against

the "principles of natural justice morality, good conscience."

        Pleading that since most of the kin of the Ghadarites do not have

any documentary proof of their ancestors participation in the freedom

struggle, but being active in research on the same for past 30 years in

which he has collected authentic material, Mr. Waraich prayed that he

should be attached with the identification process of the kins of

Ghadarites.
