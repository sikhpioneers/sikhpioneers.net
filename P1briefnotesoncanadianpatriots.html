VERY BRIEF NOTES ON CANADIAN PATRIOTS

Return to:

Main Page   

  • Bhai Bhag Singh and Bhai Battan Singh were murdered by Bela Singh in the Sikh Temple on September 5 th , 1914.
  • Bhai Mewa Singh killed William Hopkinson. He was hanged on 11 th . January, 1915.
  • Bhai Balwant Singh was to be hanged in Lahore on March 17 th , 1917 but with the special order of Michael O'Dwyer, the Lt. Governor of Panjab, he was hanged one day earlier on March 16, 1917.
  • O'Dwyer was shot dead by Shaheed Udham Singh in Caxton Hall, London .
  • Bhai Uttam Singh Hanns played a very important role during the crisis of Komagata Maru between May 23 and July 23, 1914. He was hanged in Lahore First Supplementary conspiracy Case.
  • Pandit Sohan Lal was the treasurer of the Shore Committee during the crisis of Komagata Maru. He was hanged in Mandley ( Burma ) Conspiracy Case.
  • Babu Harnam Singh was the editor of ‘Swadesh Sewak', first Punjabi paper published in North America continent in 1910. He was the owner of Swadesh Sewak Home – 1632, 2 nd Avenue, West Fairview , Vancouver . During Komagata Maru crisis, he was arrested in Sumas (U.S.A.) with Bhai Bhag Singh and Bhai Balwant Singh. Bhai Bhag Singh and Bhai Balwant Singh were allowed to enter Canada but Baboo Harnam Singh was deported and was not allowed to return to Canada . He was hanged in Mandley ( Burma ) Conspiracy Case.
  • Master Udham Singh Kasel was a close friend of Shaheed Mewa Singh. Later on he was appointed instructor to train revolutionaries, going to India , at the farm of Baba Jawala Singh Thatian, in California (U.S.A.).
  • He was given a sentence of life imprisonment in Lahore Conspiracy Case. He jumped out of the running train while shackled and handcuffed. He was killed in Sarhadi area.
  • Baboo Kapur Singh Mohi was imprisoned for life in Mandley ( Burma ) Conspiracy Case.
  • Bhai Hardit Singh Lamen Jatpura was also imprisoned for life in Mandley ( Burma ) Conspiracy Case.
  • Bhai Kartar Singh Nawan Chand met Bhai Gurdit Singh aboard Komagata Maru and informed him about the activities of William Hopkinson. He was imprisoned for life in Lahore Second Supplimentary Conspiracy Case. He left for India with Bhai Balwant Singh and his family. Bhai Balwant Singh sent his family to India with Kartar Singh from Singapore and himself went to Siam ( Thailand ) to lead the revolution, where he was arrested in Bangkok .
  • Bhai Ram Singh Dhuleta had property worth 1,85,000 in Vancouver . He was one of those who laid the foundation stone of Sikh Temple , Abbotsford. When he joined the Ghadar Party, he donated ten thousand dollars cash in American currency. He killed Pandit Ram Chandra in San Francisco Court and he was shot dead by Marshal of the court.
  • Bhai Piara Singh Langeri was the first priest of Sikh Temple Victoria . He was imprisoned for life in Lahore Conspiracy Case.
  • Bhai Karam Singh Daulat Pur left Abbotsford in the end of 1913 for India to join the Ghadar Party. He was arrested and interned in his village for years. During Babbar Akali Movement he was a prominent leader of the Babbar Movement and editor of their paper. He was killed by the British Army in Bubely Historical Encounter.
  • Bhai Sher Singh Vain Poin ( Lahore ) was also imprisoned for life and sent to Andeman prison.
  • Bhai Bhagwan Singh was deported from Canada in 1913. He returned from Japan to U.S.A. and was elected the president of Ghadar Party. He was imprisoned for 18 months in San Francisco Case.
  • Tarak Nath Dass was a great revolutionary. He was the real mentor of the political movement in Vancouver . He taught the political ideology to the mill workers. On the report of William Hopkinson, he was deported from Canada . He was imprisoned for 22 months in San Francisco Case.
  • Baba Naranjan Singh Pandori, landed in Canada in 1907 and worked on the rail line from Victoria to Nanaimo as a foreman. He joined Ghadar Party in the end of 1913 and left for India . He came back to U.S.A. in 1916. Then in 1934 he left for Moscow with Nidhan Singh Maheshri, Reja Singh Kharaudi of Arjantina and two others. He returned to India in 1942 and was imprisoned for sometime.
  • Bhai Dalip Singh Phahla was wounded in Sikh Temple , Vancouver by Bela Singh on 5 th September, 1914 and survived for and left for U.S.A. He took a very important part in the freedom movement.

Bhai Mit Singh Pandori was the General Secretary of The Khalsa Diwan Society, Vancouver at the time of Komagata Maru crisis.

He acted for Bhai Bhag Singh, the president of Khalsa Diwan Society and a charterer for Komagata Maru in his absence. Bhai Bhag Singh was locked up in Sumas (U.S.A.)

He acted as a priest and met Bhai Mewa Singh in his prison before hanging.

After the martyrdom of Bhai Mewa Singh, Bhai Mit Singh performed the last prayers before his cremation. Bhai Mit Singh was acting as a priest of the Sikh Temple , Abbotsford in 1915. On October 3, 1915, Pratap Singh Tatooan Wala (one of the stooges of Bela Singh) tried to kill him. Partap Singh missed his shots. Bhai Mit Singh snatched his revolver and gave him good beating. For this offence, Pratap Singh was imprisoned for four years.

He was the first representative of Khalsa Diwan Society, Vancouver, who was elected executive member of the first Shiromani Gurdwara Prabandhik Committee, Amritsar .

 

Source: Singh, Kesar. Canadian Sikhs and Komagata Maru Massacre (Part One). Surrey, B.C. Canada: 1989.pgs.9-12.
