Komagata Maru 'revolutionists'
Chandigarh, May 30

          The High Court today issued notice on the Public Interest Litigation seeking directions to the Union of India to include the names of Komagata Maru 'revolutionists' in the list of freedom fighters.
          In his petition, Advocate Malwinder Singh Waraich submitted that Schedule 1 of Swatantra Sainik Samman Pension Scheme does not include these revolutionists for award of pension, which should be done immediately. Going into the background of the case, he mentioned that Komagata Maru was a ship which carried 372 Indians to Canada in 1914.
          However, they were refused entry in the ship from May 21, 1914 to July 25, 1914. They were later provided ration for the return journey, following which the ship arrived in Kolkata on September 29, 1914. They were fired at on their return and several of them got injured while being arrested. Later, they were arrested and jailed.
          Taking up the petition, the court issued notice of motion for July 23.

Notice to Union Government

          The division bench of chief justice Binod Kumar Roy and justice Vigyan Mohan Jain have issued notice to the Union government for July 23, on a writ petition filed by Malwinder Jit Singh Waraich, seeking freedom fighters benefits for the legal heir of the Kamagata Maru ship incident of 1914, where a number of Indians had sailed to Canada but were refused entry. On return to India, majority of them died or suffered at the hands of the British. Though the contribution Kamagata Maru martyrs in the freedom struggle could hardly be over-emphasized, they were acknowledged in 1975 when the site for the All India Congress Committee session held at Mataur near Mohali was named after Kamagata Maru.

Notice issued

          Taking up a public interest litigation seeking directions to the Union of India against the denial of freedom fighter status to Kama Gata Maru revolutionists, a Division Bench of the High Court has issued notice of motion for July 23. Copies of newspaper reports published in The Tribune in 1914 were also annexed along with the petition.
          In his petition, Malwinder Jit Singh Waraich of Saketri had earlier stated that the Kama Gata Maru movement had not been included in the Swatantarta Sainik Samman pension scheme. Going into the background of the case, the petitioner had submitted that Kama Gata Maru - a ship carrying 376 Indian emigrants to Canada - was refused entry to Canada. He had added that the ship remained stranded for two months from May 21, 1914, the revolutionists were provided with just the ration for their return journey. In September 1914, when the ship reached India, the travelers were arrested or fired upon.

Source: Chandigarh. Saturday, May 31, 2003. Hindustan Times, New Delhi
