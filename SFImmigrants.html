SF Immigrants California Hindus 1916

That several Hindu residents of San Francisco and the Pacific Coast were among the three hundred East Indians who have been hanged in British India after being accused of sedition, was announced here yesterday by Ram Chandra, editor of the local HINDUSTAN GADAR, which has been described by the British secretary for India as the most dangerous of all revolutionary publications. Chandra has received the photographs of five of the most prominent of the California Hindus who went to India during the war, and who have been executed. They are: Karta Singh, Hindu aviator, who has been hanged, according to the report of British Crown Prosecutor Patnam. Karta Singh was on the staff of the Hindustan Gadar of San Francisco. He made bombs and explosives, organized political riots and helped to carry these out. He was an active revolutionary, and designed a flag for his fellow conspirators, Independent India, which was to symbolize an India free from British rule.

Kanshi Ram, contractor of Oregon. According to Crown Prosecutor Patnam, he plotted the removal of several police officers in Frozepore. He gave $11,000 to the Hindustan Gadar of San Francisco, and tock $1,000 to India with him to finance the revolution. The money was confiscated and Kanshi Ram hanged.

Nidhan Singh, who lived for twenty years in the United States and who was regarded as an influential leader, was also hanged. He successfully introduced arms and ammunition into India after the outbreak of the war and plotted several outbreaks and risings. He organized and took part in looting the British treasury at Moga Punjaub. Another Hindu of considerable standing in the eyes of his countrymen, Sohan Singh, religious leader of the 'Namdhari' sect, was also hanged. Ram Chandra says this man was arrested on mere suspicion, being considered the chief supporter of the Hindustan Gadar. Prethle Singh was another who went to death.

Ram Chandra states that 700 Hindus have been transported for life, and over 10,000 were interned in India without trial…..

Source: SF Exam Mon May 1, 1916.
