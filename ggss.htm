1912. The Guru Govind Singh Sahib
Educational Scholarships

Main Page


The undersigned, being of helping deserving Indian students of both sexes to receive liberal and professional education in foreign countries, beg to offer six scholarships, terrible for 3 years, and invited application for the same.

The conditions for the awards are as follows.—

  1. The Scholarships will be known as the “Guru Govind Singh Sahib Educational Scholarships”
  2. All properly qualified Indian ladies and gentlemen, without ….. of race, cast or creed, will be eligible for election to the scholarship.
  3. Each scholarship will be tenable for 3 years, commencing August 1912.
    Note. The period may be extended in the case of able and in…trious scholars, who finish in three years; course of study w… marked success and credit.
  4. The scholarship will not be given in money. Each scholarship will include free board and lodging, all expenses for cloths, books, stationery, medicine, postage and other requisite of student all college fees, and a small amount of pocket money during a period of three years.
    Note. Medical students will pay their own college and laboratory fees, which amount to about $175 (Rs. 525) per annum.
  5. All scholars will come from India to Berkeley (Cal.), U.S.A. on their own expense.
    Each scholar will receive third-class fare (second-class in the case of ladies) for the return voyage to India on the successful completion of the three years course of study
  6. All scholars will reside at a hostel to be called the “Guru Nanak Deva Vidyarthi Ashram”. Berkeley (Cal.), and lead a simple and abstermious life. Scholars will not smoke or use alcoholic beverages.
  7. a) Each scholar will study, at his or her choice at least one of the following subjects at the University of California, Berkeley, (Cal.)—History, Economics, English Literature, French Literature, German Literature, Spanish Literature, Japanese Literature, Civil, Mechanical or Mining Engineering, Commerce, Music, Medicine. (b) Each scholar will qualify himself or herself for one of the following degrees at the University:- M. A., M. L., M. Sc., Ph. D., M.D.
  8. A scholar, who is not diligent in study information with regard to maintain a good moral character, shall forfeit the scholarship and the return passage-money.
  9. Candidates should supply information with regard to the following points in their applications:--
    Sex, Age, Religion, Caste, Married or Unmarried? Present Occupation. Qualifications. References. Subject or Subjects of Study (selected from the list given above).
  10. All applications, with full testimonials and references, must reach Mrs. Bertha L. C. Pope, 2708 Virginia St. Berkeley, (Cal.),

SARDAR JAWALA SINGH.

Moorland, (Cal.), U. S. A., Jan 1, 1912.
